History of Hip Hop & RNB

This is for the real ones — the ones raised on bass lines and bars. From Bronx block parties to world stages, hip hop is more than music. It’s movement.

Legendary Artists

Tupac Shakur — Voice for the unheard. Poet. Icon.

Biggie Smalls — Flow master. Storyteller. Brooklyn’s finest.

Lauryn Hill — Truth in soul. Queen of conscious rap.

Kendrick Lamar — Voice of a generation. Pulitzer with purpose.

Regional Influence

  • East Coast: Born in the Bronx. Think Nas, Biggie, Jay-Z. Bars for days.
  • West Coast: Funky, loud, and raw. Tupac, N.W.A., Snoop Dogg.
  • South: Trap, bounce, flavor. Outkast, Wayne, Future.
  • Midwest: Speed and soul. Kanye, Twista, Bone Thugs.

Major Moments in Hip Hop

1973: Birth of Hip Hop

DJ Kool Herc hosts a party at 1520 Sedgwick Ave — spinning breakbeats and lighting a cultural fuse.

1986: Run-DMC Meets Aerosmith

A rock-rap remix changes everything. "Walk This Way" smashes charts — and stereotypes.

1990s: East vs West

Two coasts, two legends — Biggie & Pac. Love, war, and legacy in every verse.

2000s: Crunk, Trap, & ATL Takeover

The South takes the mic. Outkast, T.I., Lil Jon redefine the vibe.

2010s: Kendrick, Drake, Cole

The new wave hits with bars, melodies, and meaning. Sampling the past, owning the future.

2020s: TikTok & AI Rap

Hip hop hits every phone screen. Megan, Baby, Yeat, and even AI are changing the game.
