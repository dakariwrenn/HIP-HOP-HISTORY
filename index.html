History of Hip Hop

This is for the real ones — raised on tapes, turntables, and truth. From a Bronx party to a global movement, hip hop is how we speak, move, and breathe.

Legendary Artists

Tupac — A revolutionary poet with a mic. From the treatment of women to life in the streets, he covered all bases.

Notorious B.I.G — Brooklyn's king of flow and storytelling.

Lauryn Hill — Soul, truth, and unmatched elegance in rhyme. A true revolutionary in the game for women.

Kendrick Lamar — The voice of now. Fearless, sharp, essential.

Regional Influence

  • East Coast: Born in NYC. Lyricism, hunger, grit. Think Nas, Biggie, Wu-Tang.
  • West Coast: Laid-back funk and raw realness. Dre, Snoop, N.W.A, Tupac.
  • South: Bounce and trap music. Outkast, Lil Wayne, Future.
  • Midwest: Creativity and speed. Kanye, Common, Bone Thugs, Twista.

Major Moments in Hip Hop

1986: Rock Meets Rap

Run-DMC and Aerosmith drop “Walk This Way,” merging rock and hip hop on the charts. The video breaks racial and genre boundaries on MTV, pushing hip hop into mainstream America. LL Cool J, Beastie Boys, and Eric B. & Rakim begin reshaping the sound.

1990s: East vs West

A defining era of lyrical battles, raw storytelling, and cultural dominance. Biggie Smalls and Tupac Shakur emerge as icons on opposite coasts. Death Row (West) and Bad Boy (East) become powerhouses. Nas, Wu-Tang Clan, Ice Cube, and Snoop Dogg shape the golden age. Tensions rise, culminating in the tragic deaths of Tupac (1996) and Biggie (1997).

2000s: South Rises

The South becomes the heartbeat of hip hop. Atlanta leads with Outkast’s genre-blending genius, while Lil Jon brings crunk energy to clubs worldwide. T.I. coins the term "King of the South," and trap music begins its global ascent with Jeezy and Gucci Mane. Houston and New Orleans add flavor with chopped & screwed and bounce.

2010s: Conscious Meets Commercial

Lyricism returns to the spotlight with Kendrick Lamar, J. Cole, and Logic tackling race, politics, and personal struggle. Meanwhile, Drake dominates charts with emotionally driven hits and melodies. SoundCloud artists like XXXTentacion and Juice WRLD rise, bridging emo, punk, and rap. Hip hop becomes the most streamed genre globally.

2020s: TikTok & AI Influence

TikTok becomes the new radio, launching hits overnight. Artists like Ice Spice, Central Cee, and Doja Cat break through using viral sounds. AI begins remixing voices and generating fake songs, raising questions about originality. Despite tech advances, local scenes, street cyphers, and grassroots movements keep the soul of hip hop alive.
