Regional Influence

  • East Coast: Born in NYC. Lyricism, hunger, grit. Think Nas, Biggie, Wu-Tang.
  • West Coast: Laid-back funk and raw realness. Dre, Snoop, N.W.A, Tupac.
  • South: Bounce and trap domination. Outkast, Lil Wayne, Future.
  • Midwest: Creativity and speed. Kanye, Common, Bone Thugs, Twista.

Major Moments in Hip Hop

1973: Birth of Hip Hop

DJ Kool Herc throws a Bronx party and loops breakbeats — the night that changed music forever.

1986: Rock Meets Rap

Run-DMC and Aerosmith drop “Walk This Way” — breaking genre lines and taking hip hop global.

1990s: East vs West

Biggie vs. Tupac, Death Row vs. Bad Boy — hip hop's most intense rivalry. Pain, pride, and poetry.

2000s: South Rises

ATL becomes the epicenter. Crunk, snap, trap — the South runs the charts.

2010s: Conscious Meets Commercial

Drake, Kendrick, J. Cole — redefining mainstream with introspection, samples, and skill.

2020s: TikTok, AI, and Influence

TikTok births hits overnight. AI voices mimic legends. But the roots? Still strong.
