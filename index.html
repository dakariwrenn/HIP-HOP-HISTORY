Regional Influence

  • East Coast: Birthplace of Hip Hop, known for its lyricism and storytelling. Icons include Nas, Biggie, and Jay-Z.
  • West Coast: G-funk and gangsta rap origins. Legends like Dr. Dre, Snoop Dogg, and Tupac hail from here.
  • South: Crunk and trap music pioneers. Outkast, Lil Wayne, and T.I. represent this region.
  • Midwest: Diverse styles blending East and West influences. Eminem, Kanye West, and Common are notable artists.

Major Moments in Hip Hop

1973: Birth of Hip Hop

DJ Kool Herc's back-to-school party in the Bronx lays the foundation for Hip Hop culture.

1980s: Golden Age

Innovation and diversity flourish with artists like Run-D.M.C., Public Enemy, and LL Cool J.

1990s: Mainstream Breakthrough

Hip Hop becomes a dominant force in music with stars like Tupac, Biggie, and the rise of West Coast rap.

2000s: Global Expansion

Artists like Eminem, Jay-Z, and Kanye West bring Hip Hop to international audiences.

2010s: Digital Era

Streaming platforms and social media transform the industry, with artists like Drake and Kendrick Lamar leading the way.
